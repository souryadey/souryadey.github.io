Biography

Sourya Dey obtained his Bachelor of Technology (Honours) degree in Instrumentation Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur in 2014, along with the Best Undergraduate Thesis award in his department. He joined the University of Southern California (USC) to pursue PhD studies and was a recipient of the Viterbi Graduate School PhD Fellowship. Sourya is currently a PhD candidate scheduled to earn his doctorate in May 2020.

Research

My research can be summarized by the title of my upcoming PhD thesis — Exploring Complexity Reduction in Deep Learning. I developed a technique known as pre-defined sparsity to significantly reduce the complexity of neural networks with minimal performance degradation. I also received an award for developing a family of open-source synthetic machine learning datasets. My current project is on developing an open-source AutoML framework called Deep-n-Cheap to search for deep learning models and explore tradeoffs between performance and complexity. Check out this video or this article for quick summaries of my research.

My initial PhD research was on non-uniform sampling and mixed signal circuits. I then switched track and was involved in the inception of a new machine learning group at USC in 2016. I was instrumental in its growth via publishing several first-author papers, one of which won a Best Paper award, assisting in writing multiple successful funding proposals, and presenting seminars and posters at various events. Some of this work involved a hardware architecture, however, I have now moved over to the software and theoretical side. My current and future research interests include applying automated deep learning, interpretability of machine learning systems, and data science.
