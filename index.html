Biography

Sourya Dey is currently as a Machine Learning Research Engineer at Galois. He recently completed his PhD specializing in deep learning at the University of Southern California (USC), where he was a recipient of the Viterbi Graduate School PhD Fellowship. Prior to that, Sourya obtained his Bachelor of Technology (Honours) degree in Instrumentation Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur in 2014, along with the Best Undergraduate Thesis award in his department.
Recent News Jul 24: I am one of the organizers for the Information Sciences Institute Graduate Student Symposium (ISI GSS) 2020.
Apr 22: I successfully defended my doctoral dissertation titled Exploring Complexity Reduction in Deep Learning.

PhD Research

My PhD research can be summarized by the title of my thesis — Exploring Complexity Reduction in Deep Learning. I developed a technique known as pre-defined sparsity to reduce the complexity of neural networks with minimal performance degradation. I also received an award for developing a family of open-source synthetic machine learning datasets. My latest project was on developing an open-source automated machine learning (AutoML) framework called Deep-n-Cheap to search for deep learning models and explore tradeoffs between performance and complexity. Check out this video or this article for quick summaries of my research.

My initial PhD research was on non-uniform sampling and mixed signal circuits. I then switched track and was involved in the inception and subsequent growth of a new machine learning group at USC in 2016. Some of my early work involved a hardware architecture, however, I have now completely moved over to the software and theoretical side. My current and future research interests include applying automated deep learning, interpretability of machine learning systems, and data science.
